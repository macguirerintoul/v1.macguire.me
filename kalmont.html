Kalmont Mountain

What

An information + web design project meant to communicate hypothermia and avalanche safety information to users

When

April 2016

Who

Elena Hsu
Linda Shu

Where

IAT 235 at SFU

Why

Kalmont Mountain is a fictional tourism site with an emphasis on visitor safety. This project was part of an information design course (IAT 235), in which we were tasked with designing an adaptive and responsive website to deliver hypothermia and avalanche safety information to visitors. My teammates and I chose to expand the challenge by including tourism information while still successfully making safety the highlight.

The question was how to present it. We researched local and relevant tourism websites to see what tourism was all about, and crafted a sitemap which reflected the need for safety info as well as good eats and fun things to do. We put safety first on the navigation, since it was the star of this show.

We were designing for the young adult, since they have the most opportunity to be active and explore. Our visual design was consistently praised as “modern”, attracting younger audiences and avoiding the outdated look.

Our prototypes were minimal and clear – we didn’t want our users getting lost in a sea of calls-to-action or special offers on their way to finding out how to save themselves from hypothermia.

With a clear path to safety, users could now read up on both avalanches and hypothermia on desktop, tablet, or mobile. Our interaction features a button which switches between our two safety topics, and in user testing, it was understood 100% on first introduction.
