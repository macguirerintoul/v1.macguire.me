Sleepyti.me Redesign

What

An experience design project making the existing service Sleepyti.me more visually appealing and easier to use

When

Right now

Who

Elena Hsu

Where

Vancouver, BC

Why

Sleepyti.me, the “bedtime calculator”, is a service which calculates a sleep or wake time for users based on an average 90 - 120 minute sleep cycle - a good night’s sleep is 5 - 6 cycles. Users are shown what time they should fall asleep if they need to wake up at a certain time, or what time they should wake up if they were to go to bed now.

The service works, but the flow of interactions is not optimized. Furthermore, due to unreasonable interaction methods, the experience of using the service is not as fulfilling as it could be.

Are We Solving a Real Problem?

According to SimilarWeb, the website www.sleepyti.me attracts 4.4 million visitors per year. The average visit duration is a bit over 1 minute, about the amount of time it would take to use the service and get results. Judging by these numbers, we believe there is ample demand for the service and wish to improve it. 8% of the traffic comes from websites linking to the service, the greatest referrer being Reddit. According to a 2013 survey by Pew Research, 18-29 year olds make up the majority of the Reddit audience. This constitutes our target audience, coupled with our theory that post-secondary education students are most likely to use the service due to hectic schedules.

The goal of this redesign is to make the site more visually appealing, and easier to use. We identified these friction points in our original analysis.

Critique

  • Selecting time through drop-down takes far too many clicks and too much time
  • No hierarchy in type
  • To find out what time to wake up, users must go through an extra step of interaction
  • Site was optimized for mobile, but web wasn’t considered
  • The long complex process was not a pleasant user experience
  • A separate mobile site exists, but users are not redirected automatically and the interaction is still not as easy as it could be

We then looked at the goals of the service and what it could provide to the user. To help with the development of our redesign, we created a user flow which illustrated the interactions we would need to design for. The alarm shortcuts would be integrated via browser extensions, or through native clock functions on the user's mobile device.

We then began sketching an interface for a mobile application.

After we had made a sketch for each possible state of the app, we began creating a digital prototype in Sketch.

The opening screen. We chose a dark shade of blue which connotes night-time and wouldn't be too bright and painful to look at.

The "sleep at" screen, the first on the navigation bar. The time defaults to the current time, so to see results, users simply tap the calculate button.

To change the time, users can tap on it and the native iOS number pad appears.

Here, the user is entering 11pm as the bedtime. As each digit is input, it adds on to the right side of the time.

Here, the user has finished picking a time and can tap calculate to see results.

The "calculate" buton brings the user to the results page, where the optimum times are ranked. The circles to the right of each time represent a shortcut to create an alarm on the user's device. The bedtime selected by the user is shown in the top text, so if the user notices a mistake, they can pick the time again by tapping "change time".

Here is the second mode of the app, where the user selects a time they need to wake up at.

Here, the user changes their wake time to 8:30am.

Here, instead of the optimum times to wake up, the user is shown optimum times to go to sleep. The circles to the right represent shortcuts to set a reminder for the user to go to sleep.

This is the about page, where the user can find an explanation of what the app does and how it can help them. This page is third on the navigation, since users may have an understanding already from the description of the app in the App Store.

Next, we plan to finalize the mockups and create a web version.
